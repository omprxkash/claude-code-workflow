---
description: Argue against my own idea to stress-test it
---

Take the idea I just described ($ARGUMENTS if provided, otherwise the idea above)
and build the strongest possible case *against* it. Don't be polite — be rigorous.

1. **Steel-man the opposition.** Give the best arguments a smart skeptic would make,
   not strawmen.
2. **Failure modes.** Where and how does this most plausibly go wrong?
3. **Hidden assumptions.** What am I taking for granted that might not hold?
4. **Verdict.** Sort the objections into *fatal* (would sink the idea) vs.
   *survivable* (real but manageable), and say what I'd need to believe or prove for
   the idea to still be worth pursuing.

Be specific and concrete. If the idea is actually sound, say so — but only after a
genuine attempt to break it.
