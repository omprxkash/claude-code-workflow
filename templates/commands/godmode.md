Do a deep research and analysis pass on: $ARGUMENTS

Work thoroughly, not quickly. Produce a structured report:

1. **Framing.** Restate the question precisely and note any ambiguity in scope.
2. **Landscape.** The key facts, players, and current state. Cite sources.
3. **Analysis.** What the evidence actually supports, tensions and disagreements in
   the material, and second-order implications.
4. **Counterpoints.** The strongest opposing views and where they have merit.
5. **Bottom line.** A clear, honest conclusion — including confidence level and what
   would change it.
6. **Sources.** A list of what you used, each tied to the claim it supports.

Rules: distinguish what you know from what you're inferring. Flag low-confidence
claims. Don't pad — depth means rigor, not length. If you need a tool (web search,
docs) to do this properly, use it.
