# How to Price AI Workflows

This guide summarizes the key concepts, models, and steps for pricing AI workflows based on
value rather than time or effort.

## I. Core Mindset: Value-Based Pricing (VBP)

The fundamental shift in pricing is moving from charging for inputs (time/effort) to charging for
outcomes (return on investment or ROI).

Businesses pay for solutions that provide tangible value, typically by achieving at least one of
these three outcomes:
1. Saving them money.
2. Saving them time.
3. Reducing human error.

When presenting your price, your goal is to confidently walk the client through the math,
explaining the ROI so the price is seen as an investment that will pay for itself.

## II. The Core Pricing Models

While the industry lacks a single standard, two models are recommended for their simplicity,
trust-building, and scalability.

### 1. Model 1: Value-Based Pricing

- **Definition:** Charging based on the business impact — the time saved, money saved, or
  efficiency gained.
- **The Key Concept:** Value is not universal. You can charge two different prices for the
  exact same system to two different businesses because the solution's worth to each
  business varies.
- **Use Case:** Ideal for getting your foot in the door with a new client and the best starting
  point.

### 2. Model 2: Monthly Retainer

- **Definition:** A predictable monthly fee for ongoing access to your expertise and a
  defined level of service (e.g., $1,500 to $15,000+ per month).
- **Client Benefits:** Predictable costs, priority access, and consistent system improvement.
- **Your Benefits:** Stable, recurring income, long-term partnership, and less stress.
- **Recommended Style:** Milestone-Based Retainer (positions you as an AI
  Partner/Consultant, not a freelancer).
- **VBP in a Retainer:** Once trust is established, the focus shifts from maximum value on a
  single project to building trust, stacking wins, and increasing the retainer over time
  as your impact (and effective role) scales.
- **Justification for Retainer:** Ongoing maintenance, model updates, bug fixes, iteration,
  refinement, and acting as a trusted guide through their AI enablement and audit process.

## III. How to Actually Calculate Your Price

The calculation always starts with a robust discovery phase to understand the manual process
and its cost.

### 1. The Discovery Phase

You must first map out the current manual process by asking:
- How often does the process happen?
- What triggers it?
- Who are the stakeholders?
- How long does it take each time?
- What is the hourly dollar cost of that work? (Based on salary, number of employees,
  software costs, etc.)

### 2. Value-Based Pricing Calculation

The goal is to translate the savings into a number the client understands.

- **Step A: Calculate Annual Savings.**
  - Example: Process takes 1 hour/day × $50/hour × 240 working days = $12,000 annual savings.
- **Step B: Apply the 10x ROI Rule.**
  - The client should see a ten times (10x) return on their investment in the first year.
  - Calculation: Project Price = 10% of Annual Savings.
  - Example: 10% of $12,000 = $1,200 (Project Price baseline).
- **Note:** This calculation is based on direct savings and doesn't include opportunity cost
  (the additional value created by freeing employees for higher-value work), which means
  the actual ROI often compounds and is higher.

### 3. Monthly Retainer Calculation

For ongoing partnerships, the math shifts to protecting your margins.

- **Step A: Estimate Your Monthly Cost.**
  - Calculate the cost to deliver the service (your time, developer time, maintenance,
    etc.).
  - Example: Estimated Cost to Deliver = $5,000 per month.
- **Step B: Apply Target Margin.**
  - The target margin for custom service-based work is typically 50% to 70%. 50% is
    a safe baseline.
  - Calculation: Retainer Price = Cost to Deliver / (1 - Target Margin), or simply 2x your
    cost for a 50% margin.
  - Example: $5,000 cost × 2 = $10,000 per month Retainer Price.

## IV. Presenting the Investment

How you present the price determines the outcome. Never start with the price; start with the
transformation.

### 1. Presenting the Solution

Before stating the cost, ensure the client understands the outcome and all the components
included:
- Setup
- Hosting
- Testing and quality checks (QA)
- Optimization
- Client involvement (access, data, feedback)
- Documentation, Enablement, and Training
- Maintenance (if part of the scope)

### 2. Clarity on Scope

Avoid vague scopes. Clearly define what "done" means with a bulleted list of functionality
requirements.
- **Prevents:** Scope creep and back-and-forth debates.
- **Strategy:** When new features are requested, move them to a "version 2 backlog,"
  positioning yourself for the next project or scope expansion.

### 3. Handling Objections

- **The Golden Rule:** If they push back, adjust the scope, not the price. Reduce
  complexity, remove a non-essential feature, or break the project into phases. Do not
  discount your value.
- **Budget Sensitivity:** Redirect the conversation to the long-term value and monthly
  savings instead of the short-term expense.
- **Intellectual Property (IP):** The client owns the IP built specifically for them (prompts,
  data, workflows). You protect your reusable components, internal templates, and
  infrastructure used to speed up development.

## V. Expansion: Turning Projects into Recurring Income

After the initial value-based project, aim to establish a recurring relationship for stability and
peace of mind.

### 1. Recurring Service Examples

| Service | Description | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Maintenance Fees | Flat monthly fee for reliability (API changes, model updates, minor adjustments, bug fixes). | Ensures system health and provides a light retainer. |
| Optimization & Monitoring | Weekly/monthly review of logs and outputs to continuously refine prompts, tuning, and quality checks. | System gets better over time, especially for AI-heavy workflows. |
| Expansion Projects | Launching "Version 2" or new workflows identified during the initial project. | Turns a one-off build into a long-term partnership. |

### 2. Pricing Recurring Services

You can sell these services individually or as a package:
- Flat Monthly Fee: (e.g., $250 - $1,500 per month).
- Hourly Maintenance Package: (e.g., 5-20 hours per month).
- Percentage of Original Project Cost (Recommended): 10% to 25% of the upfront
  project fee.

## VI. The P.R.I.C.E. Framework

Use this 5-step internal framework to ensure consistent, strategic, and value-grounded pricing
for every workflow.

| Letter | Step | Action | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| P | Prepare | Ground yourself in Value-Based Pricing. | Outcomes, not hours. |
| R | Research | Fully map the manual workflow (frequency, triggers, time, cost to business). | Discovery Phase. |
| I | Identify the ROI | Calculate monthly/annual savings and efficiency gains. Use the 10x investment rule. | The numbers. |
| C | Communicate | Present the transformation first. Explain the solution, scope, QA, and client needs before presenting the price. | Building trust in the outcome. |
| E | Expand | Seek opportunities for continued engagement (maintenance, optimization, retainers, V2 upgrades). | Long-term partnership. |
